Quantum Computing

Quantum computing introduces a new paradigm in computation. Traditional computers, whether classical or supercomputers, rely on bits as the smallest unit of data, which can be either 0 or 1. In contrast, quantum computers use quantum bits or qubits. Qubits can exist in a superposition of states, meaning they can be both 0 and 1 simultaneously, thanks to the principles of quantum mechanics. This allows quantum computers to solve certain complex problems more efficiently than classical computers.
